## 1. Summary

Shoebox is an AI receipt organizer for the Canadian **personal (T1)** income-tax
market, rebuilt as a **native iOS app that runs entirely on the user's device**.
A user captures a receipt (photo, image, **or PDF**), and the app — using
**Apple Intelligence's on-device language model** plus the Vision framework —
automatically:

1. **reads** it and extracts the relevant details,
2. **validates** whether it would be **acceptable to the CRA** as support for a
   claim, and
3. **identifies the personal income-tax line(s)** it may apply to — one or more —
   e.g. medical, charitable donations, child care.

The user reviews, corrects, and manages their receipts in a list. The core loop
is **capture → extract → validate → match to line(s) → store → review**.

**What changed in this pivot, and why it matters:**

- **No backend, no servers we operate.** All reading and validation happen
  on-device with Apple Intelligence; there is no API, no cloud AI, no per-receipt
  inference cost, and no server to scale or secure.
- **No account creation.** There is no sign-up, login, password, or 2FA. The app
  uses the device's existing **iCloud account** for storage and per-user
  isolation.
- **iCloud storage instead of a database.** Receipts and their images live in the
  user's **private CloudKit database** via SwiftData, syncing automatically and
  privately across their own devices.
- **Privacy by construction.** Receipt images and text never leave the device for
  AI processing; they sync only into the user's own private iCloud.

We deliberately start with **personal (T1)** income tax — a broad audience with
comparatively simple, receipt-driven rules — rather than the complex
self-employed **T2125** business-expense rules (a future module, §13).

## 2. Background & context

Most Canadians who file a personal (T1) return hold receipts that support credits
and deductions — **medical expenses, charitable donations, child care, and more.**
They are legally required (Income Tax Act s.230) to **keep supporting documents
for six years**, and the CRA accepts digital records (IC05-1R1). But in practice
people:

- **lose** receipts between the purchase and filing,
- **keep the wrong thing** — e.g. a store sales slip instead of the **official
  donation receipt** a charity must issue, which won't be accepted, and
- **don't know which line** a receipt belongs to, or whether it's claimable.

An app that **captures, validates, and routes** each receipt to the right T1 line
— and tells the user when a receipt isn't acceptable — is genuinely useful on its
own. Doing it **on-device** removes the two biggest frictions of the original
product: it costs nothing per scan to run, and it asks the user for **no account
and no trust in a third-party server** with their financial documents.

## 3. Goals & non-goals

### 3.1 Goals

- **G1 — Zero-friction start.** A user opens the app and can immediately add a
  receipt. No sign-up, login, or onboarding account step.
- **G2 — Capture.** Add a receipt by **scanning with the camera, choosing an
  image, or importing a PDF**.
- **G3 — On-device extraction.** The app **extracts** the receipt's details using
  Apple Intelligence, with nothing sent off-device.
- **G4 — On-device CRA validation.** The app **validates whether the receipt would
  be acceptable to the CRA** and flags what's missing or wrong.
- **G5 — Line matching.** The app **identifies the personal income-tax line(s)**
  the receipt may apply to (**one or more**), with a confidence indication.
- **G6 — Manage.** View all receipts in a list; open, edit, or delete any —
  correcting the details, the validation result, and the matched line(s).
- **G7 — Private, synced storage.** Receipts and images are stored durably in the
  user's **private iCloud** and sync across their own devices, with no shared
  server.
- **G8 — Graceful degradation.** On devices where Apple Intelligence is
  unavailable, the capture → store → manual-edit loop still works (the user enters
  details by hand); automatic reading is simply skipped with a clear explanation.

### 3.2 Non-goals (explicitly out of scope for this MVP)

- ❌ **Any user-account system** — no sign-up, login, password, OAuth, or 2FA.
  Identity is the device's iCloud account.
- ❌ **Any backend / API / cloud AI.** No server, no Claude/remote inference, no
  cloud storage we operate.
- ❌ **Self-employed T2125 business-expense categorization** → future module (§13).
- ❌ **Computing claim amounts.** We **identify and validate** lines; we do **not**
  compute the deductible/creditable value, apply thresholds, or total a claim.
- ❌ **Province-specific credits** beyond the federal lines in §8 (future).
- ❌ **Android / web / cross-platform.** This product line is **iOS-native only**.
- ❌ **Sharing, collaboration, or multi-user accounts.** Single user = the iCloud
  account holder.
- ❌ **Export** (CSV / TXF / tax-software mapping), **bank connections**, **email
  ingestion**, **billing/subscriptions**.
- ❌ **Multi-currency handling** beyond storing what is read (default CAD).

## 4. Target user

**Primary persona — the everyday personal filer.** A Canadian who files a T1
return and accumulates receipts worth claiming: families tracking **medical** and
**child-care** costs, regular **donors**, and anyone with a shoebox of receipts
who is unsure which ones are claimable, which are CRA-acceptable, and where each
belongs. Tech-comfortable, time-poor, **privacy-conscious** — and already in the
Apple ecosystem with an iPhone and iCloud.

Out of persona: incorporated businesses; self-employed filers needing T2125
(future module); bookkeepers/accountants managing many clients; non-iOS users.

## 5. User stories

- **U1** As a new user, I open the app and start adding receipts immediately — no
  account to create.
- **U2** As a user, I can scan a paper receipt with my camera and have it saved.
- **U3** As a user, I can import an existing image **or a PDF** (e.g. an emailed
  donation receipt) instead of scanning.
- **U4** As a user, after adding a receipt I see — within a few seconds, computed
  on my device — the extracted vendor, date, and amount, **whether it looks
  acceptable to the CRA**, and **which tax line(s)** it may apply to.
- **U5** As a user, I can see all my receipts in a reverse-chronological list with
  thumbnail, vendor, amount, date, **matched line(s)**, and **acceptability
  status**.
- **U6** As a user, I can open a receipt, correct any extracted field, override the
  validation, or change the matched line(s), and save.
- **U7** As a user, I can delete a receipt I no longer want (with confirmation).
- **U8** As a user, my receipts appear on my other Apple devices automatically.
- **U9** As a user, I trust my receipts are private — the AI runs on my device and
  my data lives only in my own iCloud.

## 6. Experience flows

### 6.1 First run
1. App opens directly to the receipts list (empty state on first launch).
2. If signed into iCloud, storage/sync is silently ready. If not, the app works
   **locally** and prompts (non-blocking) to enable iCloud for cross-device sync.
3. If Apple Intelligence is unavailable on the device, a one-time, dismissible
   note explains that automatic reading is off and receipts can be entered by hand.

### 6.2 Add a receipt
1. Tap **Add receipt**.
2. Choose **Take a photo** (VisionKit document scanner), **Upload an image**
   (Photos), or **Upload a PDF** (Files).
3. The receipt appears immediately in the list in a *processing* state and is
   saved right away (never lost).
4. On-device: OCR → extract → **validate** → **match line(s)**; the entry updates
   with the details, an **acceptability status**, and the **matched line(s)** — or
   a *failed* state the user can still edit.

### 6.3 Review / edit / delete
1. Tap a receipt → detail view: the image/PDF, the **validation findings**, the
   **matched line(s)**, and all details.
2. Edit vendor, date, total, currency, tax, identifiers, the validation override,
   and the matched line(s) → **Save**. Or **Read again** to re-run the model.
3. **Delete** → confirm → the receipt and its stored image are removed (and the
   deletion syncs).

## 7. Functional requirements

### 7.1 Onboarding & identity

- **FR-ID1** No account creation, login, or password anywhere in the app.
- **FR-ID2** Storage and per-user isolation come from the device's iCloud account
  (private CloudKit database). One iCloud account = one private shoebox.
- **FR-ID3** The app is fully usable signed-out of iCloud, storing locally; it
  surfaces a non-blocking prompt to enable iCloud for sync/backup.

### 7.2 Capture & import

- **FR-C1** Capture via the **camera document scanner** (auto-crop, deskew).
- **FR-C2** Import from the **photo library**.
- **FR-C3** Import a **PDF** from Files.
- **FR-C4** Accept common phone image formats (JPEG, PNG, HEIC/HEIF, WebP) and
  PDF; normalize images to JPEG for consistent on-device OCR.
- **FR-C5** Store the original capture and a generated **thumbnail** for the list.
- **FR-C6** A receipt is created (in *processing*) the moment its image is saved,
  before reading completes — so a read failure never loses the receipt.

### 7.3 On-device extraction, CRA validation & line matching

- **FR-AI1** When a receipt is added, the app automatically reads it on-device
  (Vision OCR → Apple Intelligence). **No network call is made for reading.**
- **FR-AI2** **Extract** these details (any may be absent/illegible, left blank):
  vendor/payee, transaction/issue date, total + currency (default CAD), tax amount
  (GST/HST), a short description, and **document-type identifiers** where present
  (charity **registration/BN number**, child-care provider name).
- **FR-AI3** **Validate CRA acceptability** into a status:
  - **acceptable** — looks sufficient to support a claim;
  - **needs attention** — sufficient details missing/unclear, with **specific
    reasons**;
  - **not a tax receipt** — not eligible / not a receipt.
  Validation is **conservative**: when unsure, flag for attention.
- **FR-AI4** **Match to line(s)** — one or more — each with a confidence
  indication, from the supported set (§8) plus an **"Other / Uncategorized"**
  fallback.
- **FR-AI5** Extraction, validation, and matching are **suggestions, not
  determinations**: the user can correct any detail, override the validation, and
  change the matched line(s). The app retains the model's original output
  alongside the user's final values so accuracy can be measured over time.
- **FR-AI6** If the file is unreadable or clearly not a receipt, it is still saved
  in a *failed* state and is fully editable by hand. The user can **Read again**.
- **FR-AI7** Target on-device reading time **≤ 10s p50** on supported hardware.
- **FR-AI8** The line taxonomy + validation criteria are an **extensible module**
  (add a line code + its CRA criteria) without changing the capture/review UX.
- **FR-AI9** When Apple Intelligence is unavailable, reading is skipped: the
  receipt is saved for manual entry, and the UI explains why (FR/G8).

### 7.4 Receipt management (list / edit / delete)

- **FR-R1** List the user's receipts, newest first, with thumbnail, vendor, total
  + currency, date, **matched line(s)**, and **acceptability status**.
- **FR-R2** Open a receipt to a detail view with the full image/PDF, the validation
  findings, the matched line(s), and all details.
- **FR-R3** Edit any extracted detail, the validation override, and the matched
  line(s); saving keeps the user's corrected values.
- **FR-R4** Delete a receipt (with confirmation); deletion removes the record and
  its stored image and syncs the removal.
- **FR-R5** Empty, processing, and failed states are all handled in the UI.

## 8. Supported personal income-tax lines (MVP module)

Federal **T1** lines for the most common, clearly receipt-driven personal claims,
plus an **"Other / Uncategorized"** fallback. For each line, the on-device model
checks the listed **CRA acceptability criteria** (FR-AI3) when matching (FR-AI4).
The set is **extensible** (`Shoebox/Models/TaxLine.swift`).

| Line | Category | Form | Typical receipts | Key CRA acceptability criteria |
|------|----------|------|------------------|--------------------------------|
| 33099 / 33199 | **Medical expenses** | Schedule 1 (METC) | Prescriptions, dental, vision, practitioners, devices, premiums | Eligible expense; payee/provider, patient, date, amount, description of service |
| 34900 | **Donations & gifts** | Schedule 9 | Charitable donations | **Official donation receipt** from a **registered charity**: name + **registration (BN) number**, date, **eligible amount**, the words "official receipt for income tax purposes", and the CRA website. A plain sales slip is **not** acceptable. |
| 21400 | **Child care expenses** | T778 | Daycare, nanny, day camp, before/after-school | Provider name, amount, date; the provider's **SIN** if an individual |
| 31350 | **Digital news subscription** | — | Qualifying Canadian digital news subscriptions | Subscription to a Qualified Canadian Journalism Organization (QCJO); vendor + amount |
| 21900 | **Moving expenses** | T1-M | Movers, transport, temporary lodging (eligible move) | Standard receipt fields + expense type tied to the move |
| 31285 | **Home accessibility expenses** | Schedule 12 | Renovations for seniors / persons with disabilities | Standard receipt; eligible dwelling / qualifying individual; description of work |
| 40900 | **Federal political contributions** | — | Contributions to a registered federal party/candidate | **Official contribution receipt** with the registered entity + amount |
| — | **Other / Uncategorized** | — | Anything the system can't confidently place | — |

> We **identify and validate** lines; we do **not** compute claim amounts.
> Thresholds (e.g. the medical-expense floor), donation tax-credit math, and
> province-specific amounts are out of scope (§3.2).

## 9. Non-functional requirements

- **Performance:** reading ≤10s p50 on supported hardware (FR-AI7); the list
  scrolls smoothly for hundreds of receipts (thumbnails are small; full images use
  external storage and load lazily).
- **Privacy:** receipt images and text are **never sent off-device** for AI
  processing. Data syncs only into the user's **private** iCloud database. The app
  declares no data collection / no tracking.
- **Conservative validation:** the acceptability check must **err toward "needs
  attention"** — it's guidance, not a guarantee or tax advice.
- **Reliability:** an AI-reading failure never loses a receipt — it lands in a
  *failed* state and stays manually editable.
- **Offline-first:** capture, reading, and editing work with **no network**.
  iCloud sync resolves opportunistically when connectivity returns.
- **Accessibility:** Dynamic Type, VoiceOver labels, and sufficient contrast.

### 9.1 Data residency note

The original product mandated Canadian data residency for taxpayer data. With
**iCloud/CloudKit**, residency is Apple-managed and **cannot be pinned to
Canada** by the app. This is an accepted MVP trade-off given the privacy gain of
fully on-device processing and the user's own iCloud as the only store. Revisit if
a residency guarantee becomes a requirement (§13).

## 10. Cost & monetization note

Running the AI **on-device removes per-receipt inference cost entirely** — the
single biggest cost risk of the original product. There is no server to run.
Remaining costs are an Apple Developer Program membership and (only if a user
exceeds free iCloud) the user's own iCloud storage. Monetization (e.g. a one-time
purchase or subscription for power features) is deferred; the MVP is free of
metering and scan caps.

## 11. Success metrics

Because there is no backend, metrics come from **on-device, privacy-preserving
signals only** (e.g. opt-in aggregate analytics or local debugging) — never
receipt content:

- **Activation:** % of installs that add ≥1 receipt.
- **Core-loop completion:** % of captures that successfully read, validate & match
  (vs. land in *failed*).
- **Line-match quality:** % of receipts where the user did **not** change the
  matched line(s); field-correction rate (AI baseline vs. saved values).
- **Validation usefulness:** % of "needs attention" flags the user acts on.
- **Latency:** p50/p95 on-device reading time.

## 12. Key decisions

- **D-1 No accounts.** Identity = the device's iCloud account; no sign-up/login.
- **D-2 No backend.** All reading/validation on-device; storage in private
  CloudKit. No server, no cloud AI.
- **D-3 On-device AI = Apple Intelligence + Vision.** Text-only model, so Vision
  OCR feeds the language model; guided generation yields structured output.
- **D-4 PDF receipts in scope** (FR-C3) — first page rendered for OCR.
- **D-5 Multi-line matching.** A receipt may apply to **one or more** lines.
- **D-6 Personal (T1) first.** T2125 is a future module.
- **D-7 Validate, don't compute.** Flag acceptability and match line(s); no
  claim-amount math.
- **D-8 Graceful degradation.** Unsupported-AI devices fall back to manual entry.
- **D-9 Data residency relaxed** for the MVP (§9.1).

## 13. Out-of-scope → future PRDs

**T2125 self-employed module**, province-specific credits, **claim-amount
computation & T1 export**, sharing/Family-style multi-user, Apple Intelligence
**multi-receipt-per-file** detection, in-app PDF page navigation, Shortcuts / Share
Sheet ingestion, a "needs attention" smart filter, widgets, and a possible
data-residency guarantee. The taxonomy and validation rules stay **extensible** so
these slot in without disrupting the capture→review experience.
